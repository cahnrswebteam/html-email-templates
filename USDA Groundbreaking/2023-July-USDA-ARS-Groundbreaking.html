Please join us in breaking ground for the new USDA-ARS Plant Sciences Building, Aug. 1, 2023, at Pullman, Washington.

On behalf of the United States Department of Agricultureâ€™s (USDA) Agricultural Research Service (ARS), Washington State University (WSU), and the WSU College of Agricultural, Human, and Natural Resource Sciences, it is my pleasure to invite you to join USDA Secretary Thomas J. Vilsack at the groundbreaking ceremony for the new ARS Plant Sciences Building in Pullman, Washington. The new building will be located on the WSU campus and will house both ARS and WSU researchers focused on improving the health, sustainability, and profitability of dryland and irrigated agriculture in the Pacific Northwest.

This event will celebrate past, present, and future cooperative research between ARS and WSU. It will be held on Tuesday, August 1, 2023, on the WSU Pullman campus. Guests are invited to arrive at 11:30 a.m. PDT and the program will begin around 12 noon. A reception with light refreshments will be available as guests arrive.

Collaborations between WSU and ARS, the chief in-house research agency of USDA, began in 1931 and continue to exemplify one of the strongest federal-state partnerships in the nation. When completed, the new building will provide cutting-edge facilities for four ARS research units where scientists will investigate wheat health, genetics, and quality; grain genetics and physiology; sustainable Pacific Northwest agroecosystems; and plant germplasm introductions and evaluations. Members of the WSU Departments of Plant Pathology, Crop and Soil Sciences, and Horticulture will share lab and office space with federal researchers. The collaborative research between ARS and WSU will benefit growers, commodity groups, agricultural businesses, and U.S. consumers who rely on this partnership to find solutions to agricultural and environmental problems.
